📋 Overview

Project Overview

The EV3 Driving Base is a two-motor robot controlled through EV3 Classroom's ready-made programs. You'll build it, run the stock programs, observe and record results.

🎯
Learning Objectives
Understand how a two-motor differential drive works. Use EV3 Classroom's stock movement blocks. Observe how settings like speed and distance affect robot behaviour. Record and reflect on your results.
⚙️
How It Steers
Two large motors — one per wheel — run independently. EV3 Classroom's Move Steering block controls both at once. Steering value 0 = straight; negative = left; positive = right.
📦
Stock Programs
EV3 Classroom comes with pre-built driving programs in the Driving Base unit. You will use these as your starting point, adjust settings, and document what changes.
📝
Student-Driven
You decide what settings to try and how to tune them. There's no single correct answer — experiment, measure each result, and write down what you notice.

🔧 Build Guide

Build Steps

Work through each step in order. Take photos after each stage — these are part of your documentation.

01
Plan Your Layout
Sketch a top-down view of your robot before touching any pieces. Decide where the brick, motors, and wheels go. The centre of gravity should sit between the two drive wheels.
💡 Keep it compact and low. Sketch it — that sketch is part of your documentation.
02
Mount Both Large Motors
Attach the two large servo motors symmetrically on opposite sides of the chassis. Both output shafts must be on the same horizontal axis or the robot will drift when driving straight.
💡 Use a beam across both motors to lock them at the same height. Uneven motors = uncontrollable drift.
03
Attach the Drive Wheels
Press the large traction wheels onto the motor axles and lock them with a bushing so they don't slip. Both wheels must be the same size.
💡 Check that both wheels spin freely by hand before moving on.
04
Add the Caster Wheel
Mount a small caster at the front or rear. It must touch the ground evenly and let the robot pivot without scraping. Use spacers to match the height of the drive wheels.
💡 If the robot tips forward or backward, adjust the caster height first.
05
Mount the EV3 Brick
Secure the EV3 brick to the chassis so the screen faces up and motor ports B and C are accessible. Centre it between the wheels to keep the weight balanced.
💡 The brick is the heaviest part. Off-centre placement causes drift that programming can't fix.
06
Connect the Motor Cables
Plug the left motor into Port B and the right motor into Port C. Route cables so they can't catch in the wheels when the robot moves.
💡 EV3 Classroom's stock programs expect B = left, C = right. Swapping them will make the robot turn the wrong way.
07
Manual Roll Test
Push the robot by hand on a flat surface. It should roll straight without veering. If it drifts, fix the mechanical issue now — no program will correct a physical misalignment.
💡 Document anything you adjusted here. It's part of your engineering process.
